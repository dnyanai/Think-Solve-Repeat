### Think-Solve-Repeat
I like problem-solving, and though AI can offload coding tasks now (not completely!), I still feel the human brain is a complex and intriguing entity. Last time I checked, I had one (lol). So this repo is all the problems I solve/solved here and there using whatever flavor of tech stack I use. The pattern of my approach is foundational — use my brain and get my hands dirty with tinkering!

### 🧠 My Approach: Think → Solve → Repeat
Every problem in this repo follows a pattern. It's not about getting the "right answer" fast — it's about how I think through it.
In my work experience, I've found myself stuck thinking deeply on the task at hand while also worrying about my JIRA ticket. I personally and professionally have, and still do, geek out way too much than some people prefer!
I am stupid. And that's why I am a very sincere, don't-know-how-to-give-up human who loves data and every role related to data. (I have banged my head trying to figure out the roles and worked in a handful of them as well! "I am proud to share..." that I have found out which roles I am a good fit for)
I mostly use AI to learn more optimized solutions rather than getting boilerplate code, though I don't really mind it as long as collectively it doesn't waste my time. 

I like to write and still think creatively: my brain > AI brain. 

### 🤔 Why This Exists
This isn't a cheat sheet. It's a thinking log.
If you're here, you probably care about actually understanding things — not just memorizing answers to pass an interview. Same.
If anything here helps you or sparks an idea, that's awesome. If you spot a mistake or have a better approach, open an issue — I'm always learning.
